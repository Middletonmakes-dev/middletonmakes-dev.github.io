SCOUT UAV

Conventional VTOL airframe — CAD, tooling, and composite build under tight timelines.

Overview

SCOUT is a conventional fixed-wing VTOL airframe designed to a detailed client specification. The platform combines a composite fuselage, foam-core wings, and a foam-core / 3D-printed empennage to create a clean, modular structure optimised for the client’s proprietary avionics and payload stack.

To deliver the geometry and surface quality required on a compressed schedule, this project pushed a lot of tooling development in-house. It required building a dedicated 4-axis hotwire cutter for wing and tail cores, 3D-printing plugs and fixtures, and then taking the foam surfaces through additional subtractive work on the CNC before glassing with fabric.

The scope for SCOUT was airframe-only: the client retained responsibility for all systems integration, avionics, and flight testing. My role covered the full airframe workflow—from translating spec into CAD, through digital tooling, 3D printing, hotwire cutting and CNC machining, to hands-on support during composite layup and construction.

My contributions

  • Translated the client’s performance and packaging requirements into a full airframe CAD model.
  • Defined fuselage, wing, and empennage structure, including foam-core strategy and join interfaces.
  • Developed and used in-house 4-axis hotwire cutting capability for wing and tail cores.
  • 3D-printed plugs, fixtures, and small structural parts for the fuselage and empennage.
  • Programmed and ran CNC operations for subtractive finishing of foam wings and plugs.
  • Assisted with production of composite tooling and molds under a compressed timeline.
  • Supported composite layup and glassing of wings and control surfaces.
  • Shop-floor support during assembly: fit checks, minor design tweaks, and build documentation.

Technical snapshot

  • Wingspan: 1.95 m
  • Length: 1.3 m
  • Maximum takeoff weight (MTOW): 6.5 kg
  • Propulsion: Twin electric tilt-rotor VTOL
  • Navigation / autopilot stack: Proprietary to client
