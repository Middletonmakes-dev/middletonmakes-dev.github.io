ENDURA UAV

Experimental delta-wing UAV — CAD, composites, testing and full systems integration.

Overview

ENDURA is an experimental medium-endurance delta-wing UAV developed for rapid deployment and compact field operations. My role covered the entire airframe lifecycle: ground-up CAD design, machining of tooling and structural components, 3D-printed parts, full hardware integration, and custom Li-ion pack development built directly into the wing.

During this project I also created several supporting tools that shaped our performance and design decisions, including a dedicated motor bench testing platform, a temperature-logging sensor module, a UAV launcher rail for predictable takeoffs, and a slow-motion millisecond timer used to refine motor spool-up behaviour. ENDURA also marked the start of our resin-infusion workflow, a process I later managed fully: layups, tooling prep, and vacuum infusion.

My contributions

  • Full airframe CAD design: structure, internal layout, and integrated battery cavities in the wing.
  • Machining of molds, foam cores, jigs, and structural components.
  • 3D printing and iterative prototyping of mounts, ducts, fairings, and internal mechanisms.
  • Custom Li-ion battery design and assembly tailored to the 2 kg battery mass budget.
  • Motor/prop testing on a custom bench: thrust, efficiency, airflow and thermal behaviour.
  • Data logging, analysis, and iteration to refine cooling and power system configuration.
  • Development of supporting tools: motor test bench, temperature logger, launcher rail, and slow-motion timer.
  • Composite work: resin infusion, layup planning, vacuum bagging, and structural curing.
  • Complete hardware integration: iNav on Matek H743, servos, power distribution, antennas, and internal routing.
  • Shared flight-test responsibilities: launch, tuning, log review, and endurance trials.

Technical snapshot

  • Wingspan: 2.2 m
  • Length: 0.92 m
  • Maximum sensor payload: 0.5 kg
  • Maximum takeoff weight (MTOW): 3.8 kg
  • Battery mass budget: up to 2 kg
  • Propulsion: Single pusher electric
  • Endurance target: approximately 1.5 hours (mission and configuration dependent)
  • Navigation / autopilot stack: iNav on Matek H743
