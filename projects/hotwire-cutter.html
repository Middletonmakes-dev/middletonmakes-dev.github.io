CNC Hotwire Cutter

Custom foam-cutting rig for wings and fuselages, running CNC control and a dynamic wire tensioner.

Overview

This CNC hotwire cutter is built for shaping XPS/EPS foam cores for UAV wings and fuselages. The machine is driven by an MKS Gen L controller running G-code and was designed as a flexible platform for experimenting with wire tension, temperature and cutting strategies. The mechanical concept is based on RC Keith’s hotwire cutter tutorial, adapted and scaled to suit larger UAV cores.

Over time the rig evolved from a simple manual-tension setup into a fully dynamic wire tensioner system using an ESP32, load cell and stepper motor. This upgrade keeps wire tension consistent as the wire heats, cools and expands, which noticeably improves surface finish and accuracy on long cuts.

My contributions

  • Concept development and overall machine architecture tailored to UAV wing and fuselage cores.
  • Full CAD design of the frame, carriages, gantry components and wire support hardware.
  • Selection and sourcing of V-slot extrusion, wheels, hardware and motion components.
  • 3D printing of custom brackets, wire mounts, motor plates and sensor holders.
  • Machining and assembly of structural parts, jigs and alignment fixtures.
  • Electronics integration: MKS Gen L controller, stepper drivers, 24 V PSU and inductive proximity sensors.
  • Design and integration of the ESP32-based dynamic wire tensioner using a load cell and stepper motor.
  • Configuration of firmware, G-code workflow and CNCjs control for day-to-day cutting.
  • Testing and tuning of wire temperature, feed rates and foam types to dial in cut quality.
  • Ongoing tweaks based on real projects: wings, fuselage blanks and composite core experiments.

Mechanical & electronics

  • Frame sized to handle both individual wing panels and full fuselage foam blocks.
  • V-slot extruded aluminium rails with wheel carriages for smooth, easily adjustable motion.
  • Stepper-driven axes controlled via an MKS Gen L CNC controller.
  • Nichrome cutting wire with adjustable current for tuning kerf width and surface finish.
  • Integrated dynamic wire tensioner (ESP32 + load cell + stepper) to keep tension consistent.
  • 24 V power supply sized to handle wire heating and stepper loads.
  • Inductive proximity sensors for homing, soft limits and safe repeatable cuts.

What the rig is used for

  • Cutting wing cores to specific airfoils for fixed-wing UAVs.
  • Producing fuselage blanks that are later CNC-milled or hand-shaped.
  • Process experiments: wire temperature vs. feed rate vs. foam density and composition.
  • Creating foam cores for composite parts with resin-infused skins.
  • General foam prototyping where consistent, smooth cuts matter.
