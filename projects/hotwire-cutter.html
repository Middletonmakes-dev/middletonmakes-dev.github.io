CNC Hotwire Cutter

Custom foam-cutting rig for wings and fuselages, running CNC control and built to experiment with wire tension.

Overview

This CNC hotwire cutter is built for shaping XPS/EPS foam cores for UAV wings and fuselages. The rig is driven by a controller running G-code (CNCjs on a Raspberry Pi) and was designed as a flexible platform for experimenting with wire tension, temperature, and cutting strategies.

Mechanical & electronics

  • Frame built to accommodate wing panels and full fuselage blocks.
  • Stepper-driven axes controlled via a CNC controller (e.g. MKS Gen L) and CNCjs on a Raspberry Pi.
  • Nichrome cutting wire, powered with adjustable current for tuning cut quality.
  • Future integration with a dedicated wire tensioner (ESP32 + load cell / stepper concepts).

What the rig is used for

  • Cutting wing cores with specific airfoils for fixed-wing UAVs.
  • Fuselage blanks that later get CNC-milled or hand-shaped.
  • Process experiments: wire temp vs feed rate vs foam type.
  • Supporting composite work (foam cores for resin-infused skins).
