Portal PC Build

Custom small-form-factor PC with a Portal-inspired chassis and animated ESP32 “eye” display.

Overview

I wanted a custom small-form-factor PC that felt more like a prop from Portal than a standard desktop case, so I designed my own chassis around that idea. The layout, proportions, and mounting strategy were all worked out in CAD, iterating through multiple configurations until everything — GPU, cooling, PSU, and cabling — fit cleanly. The final build uses an aluminium frame and panels with 3D-printed shells, sanded and finished for a clean, almost “test lab” aesthetic.

What really brings the build to life is the circular front display. It’s driven by an ESP32 and GC9A01 round TFT panel, and it behaves like a Portal-style robotic eye: it blinks, makes small saccades, corrects its gaze, and reacts to the PC’s thermal state in real time. Behind the scenes, the system reads CPU and GPU temperatures sent over serial, renders them as a smooth HUD, and automatically switches between “eye mode” and “temperature mode” based on user-defined rules.

All of the behaviour is configurable at runtime via simple CFG-style commands sent over USB, using a lightweight HTML-based command centre (config dashboard). You can tweak blink speed, saccade timing, timeouts, fade curves, and temperature thresholds without reflashing firmware, making it easy to tune the eye from “subtle ambient” to “full drama”.

What I did

  • Concept, layout and full CAD design of the SFF case around real-world component dimensions and cooling constraints.
  • Design and machining of the aluminium frame and panels, including mounting points and access panels.
  • 3D printing, sanding, priming and finishing of the Portal-inspired outer shells and detailing.
  • Mechanical integration of all PC hardware: GPU, PSU, cooling, cabling and mounting inside a tight envelope.
  • Electronics design and wiring for the ESP32-driven front display and its power/USB integration into the PC.
  • Firmware development for the ESP32: eye animation system, state machine, temperature-driven behaviour and HUD rendering on the GC9A01 TFT.
  • PC-side script/bridge to collect CPU/GPU telemetry and stream it over serial to the microcontroller.
  • Development of the HTML-based command centre for live configuration via simple text-based commands.
  • Use of AI tools to accelerate firmware prototyping, then manual refinement of the logic and animations.
  • OS installation, testing and long-run stability checks with the display running as a “live status” indicator.

Electronics & PC hardware

  • Microcontroller: ESP32-C3 SuperMini
  • Display: GC9A01 240×240 round TFT display
  • Interface: USB serial link between PC and ESP32 for live telemetry and CFG commands
  • Sensors: PC temperature monitoring via software (CPU/GPU temps forwarded over serial)
  • Case: Custom aluminium frame with 3D-printed Portal-inspired shell and mounting brackets
